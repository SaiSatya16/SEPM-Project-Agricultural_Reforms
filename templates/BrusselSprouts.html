Brussel Sprouts (Brassica oleracea var. gemmifera)

Brussels sprout is a member of the Gemmifera cultivar group of cabbages, grown for its edible buds. The Brussels sprout has long been popular in Brussels, Belgium, from which it gained its name.

Early varieties can be harvested from autumn onwards, but the main cropping season is through winter, as the flavour of the sprouts is sweeter once they've been frosted.

About 1-2 inches of water are required per week. Use drip irrigation if possible to conserve water. Applying mulch around the plant also helps conserve soil moisture and reduce weed growth.Sprouts like a firm, neutral or alkaline soil.

A seed rate of 600-700 g is sufficient for the cultivation of ‘Brussels sprouts’ in one hectare area.

These green and compact sprouts weigh 7 to 8 grams. A single plant can produce 200 to 400 sprouts giving an average yield of 100 to 150 quintals per ha

The fruits remain hard as long as they stay on the trees, softening only after harvest. The yield ranges from about 100 to 500 fruits per tree.
